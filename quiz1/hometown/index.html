Surabaya, The Heroes City

Written by: Kemal Tangguh Aji Rajasa | 24 September 2025

Surabaya is the capital of East Java and the second-largest city in Indonesia. Famously known as the "City of Heroes" (Kota Pahlawan), it earned this title for the intense bravery shown by its people during the Battle of Surabaya, a key event in the nation's fight for independence. Today, it stands as a bustling metropolitan hub, serving as the primary port and commercial center for eastern Indonesia, blending its rich history with modern economic importanc

The city is renowned for its vibrant culture, diverse culinary scene, and significant historical landmarks. Notable sites include the Heroes Monument (Tugu Pahlawan), which commemorates the sacrifices made during the independence struggle, and the House of Sampoerna, a museum dedicated to the city's famous kretek (clove cigarette) industry. Surabaya also boasts a variety of architectural styles, from colonial-era buildings to contemporary skyscrapers, reflecting its dynamic growth and development over the years.

Beyond its historical significance, Surabaya is recognized for its well-organized urban planning, featuring numerous green parks and clean city streets. The city has a strong and distinct local culture, from its direct-speaking and spirited residents, known as Arek Suroboyo, to its bold and flavorful culinary scene. This combination of a proud revolutionary past and a well-managed, dynamic present makes Surabaya a unique and vital Indonesian city
